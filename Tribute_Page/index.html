Steven Paul Jobs

1955 - 2011

American Business Magnate | Industrial designer | Investor | Media Proprietor

Steve Jobs (Feb 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American businessman and inventor who played a key role in the success of Apple computers and the development of revolutionary new technology such as the iPod, iPad and MacBook.

Innovation distinguishes between a leader and a follower

The following list is a time line of Steve Jobs Life:

  • 1955- Born in San Francisco, California, United States.
  • 1974- Jobs travelled with Daniel Kottke to India in search of spiritual enlightenment.
  • 1976- Jobs, Wozniak and Ronald Wayne then set up Apple computers.
  • 1984- Jobs designed the first Macintosh.
  • 1985- He was removed from managerial duties.
  • 1985- Jobs resigned and left Apple.
  • 1990- He founded NeXT computers.
  • 1991- He married Laurene Powell, together they had three children and lived in Palo Alto, California.
  • 1996- Apple bought NeXT for $429 million.
  • 1996-The purchase of NeXT brought Jobs back to Apple. He was given the post of chief executive.
  • 2003- He was diagnosed with Pancreatic Cancer.
  • 2007- Apple successfully entered the mobile phone market, with the iPhone.
  • 2009- He underwent a liver transplant, but two years later serious health problems returned.
  • 2010- Jobs introduced the iPad – a revolutionary new style of tablet computers.
  • 2011- He worked intermittently at Apple until August 2011, where he finally retired to concentrate on his deteriorating health.
  • 2011- He died as a result of complications from his pancreatic cancer, suffering cardiac arrest on 5 October 2011 in Palo Alto, California.
